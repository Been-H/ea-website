Day 1 of Trip: Kampala, Uganda

Destinations, History and Geography

Kampala's Destinations

The Ugandan National Museum

The Uganda Museum was founded in 1908, when Governor George Wilson wanted artifacts to be preserved. This museum contains many collections of artifacts that illustrate ethnological and traditional life in Uganda. The museum will give the Olympians and Spectators a cultural glimpse of Uganda.

The Kasubi Royal Tombs

Located in Kampala, The Kasubi Royal Tombs is the burial grounds for the “Kabakas,” the traditional ruler of the Baganda people of Uganda. It is a UNESCO world heritage site and provides historical context for the rulers, people, and life of Uganda before colonization.

The Kampala Independence Monument

Located in Kampala, The Kampala Independence Monument stands at over 18 feet tall! It depicts a child being unwrapped and held to the sky, symbolic of Uganda's birth as a free and independent country. This marks the start of Uganda's development into a prominenet country today, something we are trying to illuminate. This makes it a must see destination.

The Ndere Center

The Ndere Center is a popular atrraction in Kampala. During the evenings, it hosts cultural nights complete with cultural songs and dances from ethnic groups and tribes, an information center and beuatiful walkways, and a brand new restaurant making it the perfect night out in Kampala Uganda! Showing off what makes Africa unique and special to the athletes and spectators will help to spark their curiosity for the story we will tell of Africa's development in the coming destinations on our trip.

Geography of Uganda

Uganda is a country located in East Africa; it is surrounded by Kenya, South Sudan, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Rwanda, and Tanzania. Most of the country is covered in plateaus with some hills and mountains. But, there are some cool topographical features here. In the center of Uganda, there are many tropical forests and grasslands. In the southwestern border of Uganda, there are the Ruwenzori Mountains. Finally, on the eastern side of the country, there are volcanic foothills.

History of Uganda

Uganda has been inhabited way before present-day Uganda was established. In fact, evidence shows that Uganda has been inhabited for over 50,000 years. Like many other African countries, Uganda was colonized by Western powers. In 1894, Uganda was declared as a protectorate of the British Empire. After 68 years under the rule of the British Empire, Uganda gained independence in 1962. Before British influence, Uganda had kings. These people were called “Kabakas”, and they ruled over the land of Buganda.
