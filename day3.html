Day 3 of Trip: Nairobi, Kenya

Destinations, History and Geography

Nairobi's Destinations

Nairobi National Musuem

The Nairobi Museum is one of many amazing museums of Nairobi that dives into its rich culture, stories, and history. It is described as a one stop shop for anyone looking samply the countries rich heritage and will be the perfect start for a visitors stay in Nairobi.

Nairobi Shopping Malls

Similiar to the Bore district of Addis Ababa, the amazing malls of Nairobi, Kenya will serve as a great way to remind many visitors from countires with a large shopping presence of what Kenya and East Africa really are an can be contrary to the single story. A few to check out are: he Galleria, Westgate Shopping Mall, Sarit Centre, Thika Road Mall, Garden City Mall, Two Rivers Mall, and The Hub Karen.

The Kenyatta International Conference Center

Going to the top of the KICC building will be an eye opening experience for anyone who goes. Being the tallest building in Kenya, it will show people visiting that the sky is the limit for East Africa and will be an amazing climax to the trip and story we are telling

Nairobi National Park

The Nairobi Nation Park is located just outside of it, a mere 7km away. It is home to many amazing animals indigenous to the region and Africa. It also gives a great view of the city. Ending the trip here will be a good way to put everything we have just seen in perspective and show that materialism is not the only unit of development.

Geography of Kenya

Part of Kenya is located on the East African coast and the rest is bordered by Somalia, Ethiopia, South Sudan, Uganda, and Tanzania. Kenya’s terrain consists of a low plain in the Indian Ocean and it gradually rises until the center of Kenya where there are mountains and plateaus. Most Kenyan’s live near the capitol Nairobi at an altitude of 5,500 feet. Kenya is a large country at 580,367 square kilometers and it contains two of Africa’s great lakes.

History of Kenya

Kenya has been inhabited by humans for a long time. Early human fossils have been found in Kenya that date back to nearly 1.9 million years ago! But, Kenya has been influenced by foreign powers for a long time. Europeans and Arabs have had a presence in Mombasa since the 14th century. But, Kenya had been having a worldly presence for a much longer time. Mombasa, by the time the Arabs got there, was already a famous port for spices, gold, ivory, and slaves. The Europeans started to explore into the interior of Kenya in the 19th century. Kenya gained independence in 1963.
