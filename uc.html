Ugandan Culture

How to Thrive

The People

Uganda has a very diverse culture due to the fact that the population of Uganda is extremely diverse. Uganda has over 70 different distinct ethnic groups that inhabit the land. Most Ugandans are christians, but Uganda does have many Muslims and Hindus as well. The most prominent languages in Kenya are Swahili, English, and Ganda.

DOs and DONTs

DO:

  1. Adopt A Team Mindset and Work With Others
  2. Respect Everyone's Differences

DONT:

  1. Make Promises You Can't Keep
  2. Expect Everything to Be Perfect All The Time

Food

The staple food of Uganda is matoke. In fact, it is recognized as the national dish of Uganda. Matoke is thought to be one of the oldest dishes in the world. Matoke is simply just steamed green bananas. Other popular dishes include Kikalayi (fried pork), Rolex (rolled eggs), and Muchumo (roasted meat).

Recreation

Sports are very important to Ugandans. Millions of Ugandans play and watch Soccer every year. In fact, Kampala, the capital of Uganda, is home to one of the largest sports stadiums in the world. Boxing and Wrestling are also adored by Ugandans. Uganda also produces great athletes in Track and Field. In 1972, John Akii-Bua from Uganda won a gold medal in the 400m hurdles at the Summer Olympics in Munich. Ugandans are very laid back, in general, and love to go out with friends and socialize.

Summary - How To Thrive

To thrive in Uganda, simply follow the DOs and DONTs outlined below, be respectful of others, and don't carry out or perpetuate single stories. As you will saw above, one of the common lanugages in Uganda is English. So, if you are from an English speaking country, these people might not be too different from you, so treat them as you would treat a friend back home! Knowing this much about their culture in the first place will make you come across as a much more educated person already.
