Day 2 of Trip: Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Destinations, History and Geography

Addis Ababa's Destinations

The Ethiopian National Museum

The Ethiopian Nation Museum, located in Addis Ababa, is home to numerous artifacts and exhibits illuminating the intriging aspects of Ethiopian histroy and culture making it a great first destination to get the people's feet wet. A few examples of items in the mesuem are Lucy: a partial skeleton of a early hominoid dating back 3.3 million years ago, and The Throne of Emperor Haile Selassie: an emperor of Ethiopia whose actions during the Ethiopian-Italian war illustrates Ethiopia's indepedence and determiantion to do what is right for their country.

The Meyazia 27 Square Monument

The Meyazia 27 Square Monument is a perfect depiction of Ethiopias independence, history and future. All throughout its history starting in the 1930s, outsiders attempted numerous times to tear it down or alter it, but the Ehiopians always pulled through to defend restore it. This illustrates how Ethiopia is a land dedicated to being the only ones to represent themselves, self-detirmination, and overcoming adversity to grow and develop making them a perfect country to purely and accurately represent Eastern African culture and their future.

The Bole District

A sub-city or district of Addis Ababa, Bole is considered to "modern and trendy" making it perfect for breaking sterotypes of African countries, cities, and people. It is home to tons of shopping centers, cinemas, and the Edna Mall which will be a fun way for people to get to know the fun side of Ethiopia.

Entoto Hill and National Park

The Entoto hill and National Park are situated just outside of Addis Ababa overlooking it. They offer a breathtaking view that will help anyone who visits understand the true extent of Ethiopias true colors, development, and future, contrary to any single story.

Geography of Ethiopia

Ethiopia is a land-locked country in Eastern Africa, bordered by Sudan, South Sudan, Uganda, Kenya, Somalia, Djibouti, and Eritrea. It is mainly composed of highlands and is surrounded with mountains on nearly all sides, but it also contains Plateaus and semi-arid regions. Like many regions in Africa, it is home to a plethora of Animal species: the Walia Ibex, Ethiopian Wolf, and Gelada Baboons to name a few are only found in Ethiopia.

History of Ethiopia

Ethiopia is actually the oldest nation state in Africa being founded in the 13th century! Before this time, the region it resided in was known as Abbysinia and it was ruled by kingdoms and dynasties. During the 19th and 20th century, Ethiopia avoided and defeated numerous colonization attempts by Britain, France, and Italy most notably in the 1930s and 40s during the Italo-Ethiopian Wars becoming one of few African countries to completely avoid foreign interference. What followed was a period of instability, civil wars, and famine until reforms were made. Being free of foreign interference and colonization, Ethiopia serves as a better and more pure representation of what Africa is today.

Government of Ethiopia

Ethiopia is a democracy, although it is different from many that come to mind today. Ethiopia is a parliamentary republic, meaning the prime minister is the head of the government and a Parliament exercises executive power. A prime minister is elected by the parliament itself not the people. The country is divided into smaller states based on ethnicity which has caused problems in the past.
